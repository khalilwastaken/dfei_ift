KEY FINDINGS
----------------------------------------------
1. OS tracks drive double-B TP: mask_os collapses double-B from 3.88% to 0.60%,
   identical to mask_both. Removing SS alone (mask_ss) barely changes it (2.87%).

2. Single-B is more balanced: SS slightly more important than OS for single-B,
   but both contribute. TP degrades gradually from 25.9% to 19.8% through masking.

3. ~20% residual single-B TP in mask_both comes from origin_flag not in {1,2,3,4}
   (flags 0, 5, 100 and MISSING_FLAG=-1). Confirmed by mask_all: zeroing those
   tracks flips the model into anti-tagging (WF~85%), meaning flag {0,5,100}
   tracks carry real flavour information in the correct direction.

4. mask_all anti-tagging: with only MISSING_FLAG tracks active, the model
   systematically predicts the wrong flavour. Those tracks either carry inverted
   signal or the model is driven by graph structure alone.

Note: mask_all TP reported as "anti-tag" because WF>50% makes the TP formula
value (epsilon*(1-2*WF)^2 ~ 50%) physically meaningless -- the model is
consistently wrong, not correctly tagging.
